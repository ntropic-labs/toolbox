# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Iconforge serves app builders, designers, and open-source maintainers who need to compose a simple icon and export correct iOS and Android assets from the browser. They are likely working inside an implementation flow, checking output quickly, and avoiding install-heavy design or build tooling.

## Product Purpose

Iconforge is a web-only icon generator for composing shape, text, and SVG layers, then exporting platform icon assets in a ZIP. Success means users can create a usable icon, adjust layers precisely, and trust the generated manifests and asset sizes without sending project files to a server.

## Brand Personality

Polished open source: direct, credible, restrained. The interface should feel like a real workshop tool maintained by people who care about exact output, not a teaser for a paid product.

## Anti-references

Avoid toy generator cues: oversized playful controls, novelty graphics, weak density, or visual treatments that imply unreliable output. Avoid heavy design-suite complexity: excessive chrome, buried panels, jargon, or feature surfaces beyond the icon-export job. Do not use SaaS marketing gloss, hero metrics, decorative badges, gradient text, or buzzword-driven copy.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the export job visible: creation, adjustment, preview, and export should remain easy to locate at a glance.
2. Earn trust through precision: labels, states, and controls should make exact layer and asset behavior clear.
3. Prefer restrained density: compact controls are appropriate, but never at the cost of readable labels or keyboard operation.
4. Preserve open-source credibility: show useful affordances such as repository access without turning the app into a marketing page.
5. Remove unsupported ceremony: avoid decorative UI that does not help users compose, inspect, or export icons.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No project-specific accessibility requirement was stated. Maintain standard production web practices: semantic controls, keyboard access, visible focus states, readable contrast, and reduced-motion alternatives when motion is added.
